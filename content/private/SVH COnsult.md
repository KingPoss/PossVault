## Regarding Movie Screening
1. Is this during the day, or at night? Is it inside, or outside?
2. How big is the screen, what distance can we set the projector at?
3. Is this going to be with admission, or for free? 
4. How much for a projector?
5. Is there a sound system we plan on using, or do we need one of those too?

### Things to consider
- MPLC Blanket Public Screening License - https://us.mplc.com/ , if we want to avoid breaking copyright law and opening up the park to litigation, we need some form of licensing. Status as a non-profit does not change this. U.S. Copyright Act (Title 17 of the U.S. Code) Applies to this. **"Title seventeen, United States Code, section one hundred and six, subsection four"**
- When copyright compliance organizations hear about movie nights or screenings, they'll often start sending emails, asking you to purchase licensing, and if you ignore it, they'll send compliance checks.
